Self-Initiated

Corporate GHG Inventory & Emissions Analysis — Full Scope 1, 2 & 3 Assessment Following GHG Protocol Corporate Standard

A complete 2023 corporate greenhouse gas inventory built for a fictional three-facility outdoor gear company (Denver HQ, Reno distribution center, Salt Lake City manufacturing). The workbook covers Scopes 1, 2, and 3 under the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard, using EPA Emission Factors Hub 2025 data and eGRID subregion-specific electricity factors (RMPA for Denver, NWPP for Reno and Salt Lake City). It implements dual Scope 2 reporting (location-based and market-based) and disaggregates individual greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O) across every source rather than reporting a single blended CO₂e figure. Every calculation is linked through XLOOKUP formulas to centralized activity data and emission factor tables, so updating a single factor or activity value flows automatically through the entire inventory.

The project demonstrates the technical depth required for audit-grade sustainability reporting, not just familiarity with the framework. Disaggregating gases, applying region-specific electricity factors, and building dual Scope 2 reporting are the same controls expected for CDP submissions, third-party assurance, and emerging mandatory disclosure regimes like California SB 253 and the EU's CSRD. The workbook also surfaces methodological judgment calls that show up in real inventories, including a unit-mismatch resolution for fleet gasoline (CO₂ factors keyed to gallons, CH₄ and N₂O factors keyed to vehicle-miles traveled) that required adding a secondary activity data row to reconcile. The result is a fully traceable, reproducible inventory that mirrors what a sustainability analyst or ESG reporting team would actually deliver, rather than a simplified academic exercise.
